A Brief Reminder of Our Game's Origin and Its Connection to the Theme "Bavaria"

The core concept of our game revolves around escaping the Sendlinger Tor subway station. At the beginning of the game, the player finds themselves trapped inside a train in the middle of the night.

To escape the station, the player must solve a series of puzzles. The game's objective is simple: successfully escape, and the game is complete.

The process of loading the CAD file into Blender and preparing it for our purposes was a significant undertaking. Here’s how we managed it:

Importing the CAD File: We began by importing the CAD file into Blender, which involved converting the file into a compatible format. This initial step was straightforward, but it was just the beginning.

Cleaning Up the File: The CAD file contained numerous elements and details that were unnecessary for our game model. We had to meticulously clean the file by removing extraneous elements, which was a labor-intensive task.

Fixing Vertices and Normals: One of the most challenging parts was fixing the vertices and normals. The CAD file had numerous vertices that needed adjustment to ensure the model's integrity. Correcting these issues was time-consuming and required a lot of patience and precision.

Optimizing the Model: After cleaning up the elements and fixing the vertices and normals, we optimized the model to ensure it would run smoothly in the game. This involved reducing the poly count and ensuring that the model was game-ready.

Handling Collision and Lighting Issues: After importing the file from Blender to Unreal, we encountered significant problems with collision and lighting. Initially, we attempted to import the model as USD and GLTF with textures, but this approach was unsuccessful. Consequently, we had to recalculate and remove all the textures in Blender. We assigned all vertices to groups with the same material so that we could add the textures in Unreal to the groups. To achieve this, we utilized a World Coordinate Addon.

This step of cleaning the map and importing it to Unreal, ensuring that the collisions, lighting, textures, etc., worked properly, constituted about half of the total work on the project.

Despite the challenges, including the painstaking work of fixing vertices and normals, cleaning the file from elements we didn't use, and solving collision and lighting issues, the effort was worthwhile. The accurate representation of Sendlinger Tor adds an authentic Bavarian touch to our game, enhancing the player’s experience.

From Sketchfab, we purchased a model of a C2 Train from the Munich Subway (MVG). We then modified it to include an interior and a cabin that can be interacted with, animated doors, lighting, and enhanced textures.


To enhance realism, we added characteristic assets to our level for fine-tuning. We also modified some textures to prominently display the MVG logo. Some assets were modelled by ourselves, and we also purchased some models from sketchfab or downloaded them via Quixel Bridge.
